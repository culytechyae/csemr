# Security Awareness Program Policy
## School Clinic EMR System

**Policy Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** January 2025  
**Review Frequency:** Annually  
**Owner:** IT Security & Compliance Team

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for periodic security awareness campaigns and training programs to ensure all workforce members understand their role in protecting healthcare information and maintaining security compliance with Malaffi requirements.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all workforce members including:
- Administrative staff
- Clinical staff (doctors, nurses)
- IT personnel
- Third-party vendors with system access
- Temporary staff and contractors

## 3. Policy Statement

The organization is committed to maintaining a security-aware culture through regular security awareness campaigns and mandatory training programs that educate workforce members about information security threats, best practices, and their responsibilities.

## 4. Security Awareness Campaigns

### 4.1 Campaign Frequency
- **Quarterly Awareness Campaigns:** Conducted every three months focusing on current security threats and best practices
- **Monthly Security Tips:** Distributed via email or internal communications
- **Annual Comprehensive Training:** Mandatory for all workforce members
- **Role-Specific Training:** Additional training for clinical and IT staff

### 4.2 Campaign Topics
Security awareness campaigns must cover:

#### Core Topics (All Staff)
- Password security and multi-factor authentication
- Phishing and social engineering awareness
- Physical security and clean desk policies
- Incident reporting procedures
- Healthcare information protection requirements
- Malaffi compliance requirements

#### Clinical Staff Additional Topics
- Patient data confidentiality
- Access control and need-to-know principles
- Secure handling of patient records
- HIPAA/ADHICS compliance basics

#### IT Staff Additional Topics
- Secure coding practices
- System administration security
- Network security fundamentals
- Vulnerability management

### 4.3 Campaign Delivery Methods
- Interactive online training modules
- In-person training sessions
- Email newsletters with security tips
- Poster campaigns in office locations
- Quarterly security webinars
- Phishing simulation exercises

## 5. Mandatory Training Requirements

### 5.1 New Employee Training
- **Timing:** Within 30 days of hire
- **Duration:** Minimum 2 hours
- **Topics:** All core security topics
- **Verification:** Completion certificate required

### 5.2 Annual Refresher Training
- **Timing:** Within 30 days of annual review date
- **Duration:** Minimum 1 hour
- **Topics:** Updated security policies and current threats
- **Verification:** Completion certificate required

### 5.3 Role-Specific Training
- **Clinical Staff:** Additional 1 hour annually on healthcare data protection
- **IT Staff:** Additional 2 hours annually on technical security
- **Administrative Staff:** Additional 30 minutes on data handling

## 6. Training Record Management

### 6.1 Documentation Requirements
All training activities must be documented including:
- Employee name and role
- Training date and duration
- Training topics covered
- Completion status
- Assessment scores (if applicable)

### 6.2 Record Retention
- Training records retained for minimum 3 years
- Records stored in secure HR/IT database
- Regular audits of training completion rates

### 6.3 Non-Compliance Handling
- Employees failing to complete mandatory training within required timeframe receive written warning
- Access restrictions applied until training completed
- Repeated non-compliance escalated to management

## 7. Phishing Simulation Program

### 7.1 Program Frequency
- **Quarterly Phishing Simulations:** Sent to all workforce members
- **Results Tracking:** Monitor click rates and reporting behavior
- **Education:** Provide immediate feedback to those who click

### 7.2 Metrics and Reporting
- Track phishing simulation click rates
- Monitor reporting rates (good behavior)
- Identify repeat offenders for additional training
- Report quarterly metrics to management

## 8. Security Awareness Metrics

### 8.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Training completion rates (target: 100%)
- Phishing simulation click rates (target: <5%)
- Security incident reporting rates
- Policy acknowledgment rates

### 8.2 Reporting
- Quarterly security awareness reports to management
- Annual security awareness program review
- Identification of areas for improvement

## 9. Responsibilities

### 9.1 IT Security Team
- Develop and maintain training content
- Conduct security awareness campaigns
- Track training completion
- Analyze security awareness metrics
- Update training materials based on emerging threats

### 9.2 Human Resources
- Coordinate new employee training schedules
- Maintain training records
- Ensure compliance with training requirements
- Escalate non-compliance issues

### 9.3 Management
- Ensure staff participation in training
- Allocate time for training activities
- Review and approve security awareness budget
- Support security culture initiatives

### 9.4 All Workforce Members
- Complete mandatory training on time
- Participate in security awareness campaigns
- Report security incidents promptly
- Follow security policies and procedures

## 10. Security Awareness Campaign Calendar

### Q1 (January - March)
- **Theme:** Password Security and Authentication
- **Focus:** Strong passwords, MFA, credential protection

### Q2 (April - June)
- **Theme:** Phishing and Social Engineering
- **Focus:** Email security, suspicious links, reporting

### Q3 (July - September)
- **Theme:** Physical Security and Data Protection
- **Focus:** Clean desk policies, device security, visitor access

### Q4 (October - December)
- **Theme:** Incident Response and Compliance
- **Focus:** Reporting procedures, Malaffi compliance, year-end review

## 11. Continuous Improvement

### 11.1 Program Review
- Annual review of training effectiveness
- Update training content based on:
  - Emerging threats
  - Incident trends
  - Industry best practices
  - Feedback from participants

### 11.2 Technology Updates
- Regularly update training platforms
- Implement interactive learning tools
- Leverage gamification where appropriate
- Use video content for complex topics

## 12. Compliance

This policy ensures compliance with:
- **Malaffi Security Assessment Template v3** - HR 3.4
- **ADHICS Requirements** - Security awareness training
- **HIPAA Requirements** - Workforce training on PHI protection

## 13. Policy Violations

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Access restrictions until compliance achieved
- Additional mandatory training
- Written warnings in personnel file

## 14. Review and Updates

This policy shall be reviewed:
- **Annually** as part of security program review
- **When regulations change** (e.g., ADHICS updates)
- **After security incidents** to identify training needs
- **When new threats emerge** requiring updated training
